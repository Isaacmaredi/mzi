The Book - Mzilikazi the Great

Telling the story of ...

Mzilikazi the Great is like relating the story of Southern Africa where he moved through, ruled and conquered for fourty seven years..

Over this period Mzilikazi moved through seven of the nine of today’s South African provinces and passed through nine countries in Sub Sahara before settling in Zimbabwe as the tenth country.



Ascension to throne ...

Born in 1790 in Ndinaneni close to the Mkuze valley he was raised in Zwide’s palace as his grandson.

However after Zwide murdered his father for tipping off Shaka against imminent danger he was elevated to the Khumalo throne. From here on he joined Shaka and then later defied him and started his illustrious history.



The book journey ...

To enhance the story, a total of over a hundred and seventy books were referenced; libraries, museums; heritage sites, historical sites and countries were also made use of.

Various people supported this initiative from initial stage, to the point where editing, typesetting and formatting was done by Madibana Printers in collaboration with various editors.

Mzilikazi burial site at Entumbani

The resting place where Mzilikazi is buried, the area is full of rabbits and thus Ndebeles who compared Mzilikazi with a leopard, an elephant and a rabbit at times assert that where he lies he is enjoined with the rabbits. These animals are renown for their wit, speed and quick adaptability to ever changing circumstances.

One of the War Places in South Africa

Mzilikazi is one leader that is known to have fought with the Boers over land and he is also said to have fought Portuguese (when he was now in Zimbabwe) pushing them back for abducting people and making them slaves. It was only much later that Dingaan also put up a fight against the Boers.

Direct Descendants of Mzilikazi

Molope in Bulawayo hosted by members of the Khumalo royal household. Descendants from three families of Beje, Ndonda and Matshobane. On the right, in royal outfit, is Mkhulu Leo whose grandfather was Mzilikazi‘s last born son.

One of the Many sites named after Mzilikazi

Tracks of Mzilikazi, whom the Boers corrupted to Zilkaat, can be found in various places. This near Madikwe River where Mzilikazi once settled, which the locals know as Pitsedisulejang.

Route to Mzilikazi Memorial Site

Mzilikazi created what got to be known as the roving kingdom over a twenty-year period, conquering and subjecting people to his rule as he constantly increased his force of fighters by making young men to join his army.
